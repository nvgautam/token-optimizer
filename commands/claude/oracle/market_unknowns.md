# Oracle — Market Unknown Unknowns

After market segment resolves, surface 2–3 questions from the matching section below
before Phase 2 begins. Lead with: "Before we go deeper — here are questions most
[segment] founders wish they'd asked earlier." Do not explain the checklist source.
Pick the most relevant to what the user has already described.

---

## B2C (Consumer)

1. **What's your D1/D7/D30 retention floor before you start paid acquisition?**
   Trap: founders pay to acquire before they know retention. Burned CAC reveals nothing.

2. **Who owns the relationship — you or the distribution platform?**
   Trap: TikTok/App Store algo changes = existential. Most founders don't map this risk.

3. **What happens to your unit economics when Apple/Google takes 30%?**
   Trap: web-first founders discover mobile IAP economics are broken at signature time.

4. **Have you watched 5 strangers who don't know you attempt your onboarding cold?**
   Trap: founders overestimate user patience. Activation death happens in step 2 of 7.

5. **What's your data deletion story at 1M users — GDPR + CCPA + right to erasure?**
   Trap: high-volume B2C deletion is expensive engineering; discovered late when fines arrive.

6. **What's your re-engagement strategy for users who churned in week 1?**
   Trap: most focus on acquisition; resurrection cohorts are often 3× cheaper than new users.

## SMB

1. **Who actually signs the contract — the champion, or someone above them you haven't met?**
   Trap: SMB has a hidden owner layer. Deals die when the real decision-maker says no at close.

2. **What's your strategy when the champion who bought leaves the company?**
   Trap: SMB churn driver #1. Successor doesn't know why they're paying; cancels on renewal.

3. **Have you designed for the customer who cannot afford implementation help?**
   Trap: SMB can't hire consultants. If setup takes >1 day unassisted, they cancel.

4. **What's your credit card failure rate, and do you have a dunning sequence?**
   Trap: involuntary SMB churn from failed payments runs 10–20%; most founders don't measure it.

5. **Have you modeled support cost per customer at your target price point?**
   Trap: at $49/mo, a single 30-min support ticket eats the month's margin.

6. **If your product goes down Monday 9am, what's your comms plan to 5,000 customers?**
   Trap: SMB = high volume, no account managers. No status page + proactive email = trust collapse.

## Enterprise

1. **Have you met the economic buyer, or only the champion?**
   Trap: champion ≠ buyer. Deals die in legal/procurement after months of POC because buyer never bought in.

2. **What's your security review story — SOC 2, pen test, data residency?**
   Trap: enterprise triggers a 3–6 month security review post-POC. Founders discover this after demo.

3. **What does your implementation / professional services motion look like?**
   Trap: enterprise expects hand-holding post-signature. No PS = stalled deployment = no renewal.

4. **Can you run in their VPC or on-prem, or are you cloud-only?**
   Trap: enterprise IT requires data sovereignty. Cloud-only disqualifies you before the conversation starts.

5. **Who owns the renewal relationship 6 months before renewal date?**
   Trap: churn happens when the champion changes and no one above them knows you exist.

6. **Have you stress-tested your contract against SLA penalties and liability caps?**
   Trap: enterprise procurement inserts SLA clauses founders sign without reading; first outage = legal exposure.

---

## Developer Tools / PLG

1. **What's your time-to-first-value in minutes — have you timed it with a stranger?**
   Trap: PLG dies if TTFV > 10 min. Founders count from account creation; real users count from intent.

2. **Is your free tier genuinely useful, or deliberately crippled?**
   Trap: crippled free tiers don't convert. Developers talk — word of mouth requires real value delivered.

3. **What's your plan for the developer who builds production on your free tier and forgets to upgrade?**
   Trap: enforce limits → production down → viral negative review. Need a graceful degradation path.

4. **What's your API versioning and breaking-change communication policy?**
   Trap: one unannounced breaking change destroys developer trust permanently. Most PLG tools break this.

5. **What's your motion when a developer champion needs legal/procurement to sign?**
   Trap: bottom-up PLG hits a wall when enterprise legal appears. Need a self-serve → sales handoff.

6. **Have you designed your docs for someone who finds you at 11pm with a deadline?**
   Trap: developer adoption happens under pressure. Docs that require support to succeed = no adoption.
